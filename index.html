Welcome to your Bucket List

This page was designed to help you keep distractions away and focus on a 4-step process to help you reflect and create your list of things wanted to do.

What is a bucket list?

It is a collection of goals, dreams and aspirations that you would like to accomplish within your lifetime. The basic meaning of a bucket list is to keep track of your goals and to take steps to achieving these goals in order to maximize the experiences in your life.

How to build a List

Brainstorming

Take some time to reflect before write down your list

Make it a judgement free exercise, nobody is listening
  • Choose goals that are meaningful to you.
  • Cast off expectations.
  • If you feel comfortable talk to others for inspiration.
  • Allow yourself to dream big.
  • Don’t let your answers be controlled by fear and hesitation.

Write your first Draft

Now it's time to add some items in your list
  • Write down every idea. Use the fields below to add as many items as you want to your list
  • Include a combination of large and small goals.
  • Re-order and group items by place, or date. By clicking and moving items around on the list you can put them close together
  • It is okay to remove and re-write. Just click on the beside the item to remove it from the list
  • Take some time to reflect before removing an item

Organizing and Review Your Bucket List

Take a critical look on your draft and organize the final list
  • Write down every idea. Use the fields below to add as many items as you want to your list
  • Break big goals down into easier and harder variations.
  • Group items by time. Drag and drop an item to move it around
  • Remove unrealistic items. use the to remove an item
  • Review your list frequently.

Finish and Share your list

Time for the final touches. Remeber to share this list with an adventure buddy.
  • Find a bucket list buddy share your list. Having someone to share helps to ticking off some items.
  • Share your list using the email this list form buttonon top of the bucket list, fill the form and you'll receive this list by email.
  • Review and repeat this exercise a couple of months. You may find yourself changed when you start ticking items of the list
  • Be forgiving to yourself. Sometime life gets in the way.
  • Remember to enjoy this exercise, it isn't a obligation. It's a mindful exercise to help organize your thoughts aiming for a life with fewer regrets.
